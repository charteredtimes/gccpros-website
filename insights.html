8 Harsh Truths for the
Future of GCCs

Unfiltered insights from India's GCC leaders — straight from the GCC Insider Convergence. Real voices, real accountability.

"We will never have a hub or a spoke model, we will have a hub AND spoke model — both will coexist."
1 / 9
GCC Insider Convergence · Mar 2026
Sandeep Poddar
Kimberly-Clark
LinkedIn
💡 Why it matters

The Tier 1 vs. Tier 2 city debate is over. The future of location strategy isn't binary — it's about placing the right capabilities in the right ecosystems to leverage both scale and niche talent.

"In the next 5 years, technical will be a commodity. The judgment will be the premium."
2 / 9
GCC Insider Convergence · Mar 2026
Vinod Shah
Zendesk
LinkedIn
💡 Why it matters

AI can generate half your code or automate your reconciliations. What it can't do is frame the complex business problem. System thinking and problem-framing are your new competitive moats.

"Time is not the risk. It's essentially the stagnation of skills that will be the risk."
3 / 9
GCC Insider Convergence · Mar 2026
Srinivas Sampath
Upland Software
LinkedIn
💡 Why it matters

10 years of experience means nothing if it's just 1 year of experience repeated 10 times. Longevity in a GCC only protects you if you are constantly reinventing your value.

"There is no reason why you can't do a CFO role sitting here in India."
4 / 9
GCC Insider Convergence · Mar 2026
Atul Chanani
Citi Bank
LinkedIn
💡 Why it matters

The geographic ceiling has shattered. With GCCs at the center of global data flows and transformation, global enterprise leadership roles are no longer restricted to the HQ.

"You have to start thinking of business outcomes versus just pure technical skills."
5 / 9
GCC Insider Convergence · Mar 2026
Ruchi Dubey
Metro
LinkedIn
💡 Why it matters

GCCs are looking for Business Partnering skills — the ability to translate technical output into measurable impact for global stakeholders. Being a technical wizard alone is no longer enough.

"I don't think you just need an upskilling of your skill set. I think you need right-skilling."
6 / 9
GCC Insider Convergence · Mar 2026
Sameer Shaikh
XPO
LinkedIn
💡 Why it matters

Hyper-specialization in a dying process is a trap. You don't just need to learn more — you need to learn the right things that align with where your GCC is maturing on the value chain.

"Tell yourself that you can work on strategic finance. Don't wait for anybody to tell you that."
7 / 9
GCC Insider Convergence · Mar 2026
Nitin Madan
Barclays
LinkedIn
💡 Why it matters

GCC leaders need to stop acting like execution arms waiting for a mandate. Take the initiative, start driving the strategy, and claim your seat at the table before it's explicitly offered.

"One of the most important skills everybody has to learn is learn how to learn."
8 / 9
GCC Insider Convergence · Mar 2026
Jayesh Prajapati
SLB
LinkedIn
💡 Why it matters

We are shifting from an IQ-driven era to an EQ and adaptability-driven era. The tools will keep changing — your ultimate superpower is how fast you can unlearn and relearn.

"The journey from specialist to generalist is where the real trust lies. Always add your perspective to whatever you do in a GCC."
9 / 9
GCC Insider Convergence · Mar 2026

LinkedIn
💡 Why it matters

Being a silent executioner is a career killer. To break out of the back-office stereotype, speak up, share your strategic viewpoint, and ensure global stakeholders understand the business impact of your work.
